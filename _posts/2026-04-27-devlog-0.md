I launched the Trainer Ops Google Sheets system on Etsy last week.

Yes, last week. And I'm already building an app.

I know how that sounds. But hear me out because this isn't a case of "the product isn't working so I'm pivoting." The Sheets system works exactly the way it's supposed to. It's structured, it's practical, and it solves a real problem for independent personal trainers who are trying to run a business without paying for expensive software every month.

The thing is, the moment I finished building it, I could already see what it couldn't do. And more importantly, I could see where it naturally wanted to go.

---

## The spreadsheet was always step one.

When I built the Trainer Ops Sheets system, I wasn't just making a template. I was mapping out how an independent PT actually runs their day: clients, sessions, programming, finance. The whole operational picture.

And once that picture was clear, it was obvious that a spreadsheet could only take it so far. Not because spreadsheets are bad. They're actually great for getting structure in place fast, which is exactly why I started there.

But there are things a spreadsheet will never do. It won't sync to your Google Calendar when you book a session. It won't send you a reminder before a client shows up. It won't fit in your pocket. It won't grow with your business the way an app can.

I didn't need a year of using the Sheets system to figure that out. I could see it while I was building it.

So I made a decision: the Sheets system is the foundation, and the app is where this goes next.

---

## Why Flutter, why now, and why me.

I'm a NASM-Certified Personal Trainer with a background in operations. I've spent time in roles where I had to figure out how systems work and how to make them better. That combination is basically the whole reason Trainer Ops exists. I understand the trainer's world and I understand how to build operational structure around it.

What I'm actively learning is Flutter. I want to be upfront about that. I'm building this app myself, and I'm learning the framework as I go. Some parts of the build come quickly. Other parts take longer than I'd like to admit.

But I'd rather be honest about that than pretend I'm something I'm not. The product vision is clear. The execution is a work in progress, and I'm okay with that.

---

## What the app will do.

The Trainer Ops app is being built as a local-first mobile app. That means your data lives on your device, not in someone else's cloud. One-time purchase, no monthly subscription, no ongoing fees.

Here's what's on the roadmap:

- **Client management** — all your clients, sessions, and programs in one place
- **Program builder** — build and assign training programs from your own exercise library
- **Finance and invoicing** — log payments, track revenue, stay on top of your business
- **Google Calendar sync** — schedule a session in the app and it automatically appears in your Google Calendar
- **Google Drive backup** — your data backed up to your own Drive, on your terms

For existing Sheets system buyers, there will be a one-time import feature so your data carries over seamlessly. You won't be starting from scratch.

I have a beta tester lined up, a PT who gave me the kind of feedback early on that confirmed this was worth building. His calendar sync request is already on the feature list.
